Photography Standards

Context: Why Custom Matters

This guide is built on a clear performance metric: Custom dealer-taken photography significantly outperforms OEM stock imagery.

Commercial buyers require technical verification and visual transparency. While stock photos look clean, real-world photos build immediate trust, prove the asset's condition, and qualify high-intent business enquiries faster.

Landscape Mandatory

Vertical photos provide a poor browsing experience and are down-ranked. All vehicle photos must be horizontal (landscape).

Headlight Height

Always crouch to headlight level for exterior shots. This enhances the truck's silhouette and professional dealer presence.

Standard Exterior Angles

Critical Technical Documentation

Compliance Plates: Must be shot with high-clarity zoom. GVM, GCM, and VIN must be legible. If the buyer can't read the plate, the listing is incomplete.
Service History: Photograph service books, recent major repair invoices, and oil sample records. Transparency builds immediate trust.

02 Ad Writing Guide

Truck buyers are making commercial purchasing decisions. Your ad description must function as both a technical specification sheet and a trust-building document.

Platform Intelligence

  • Carsales Business: "Descriptive words illustrate positive benefits."
  • Commercial Truck Trader: "Lead with facts, not hype."
  • AutoTrader UK: "The description builds expertise and trust."

Structure & Length

Target: 100–200 words of narrative, plus bulleted specs. Short descriptions leave business questions unanswered.

1. Opening statement: Lead with the strongest hook.
2. Key specifications: Bullet points, scannable format.
3. Condition narrative: Honest condition & history.
4. Logistics: Price, Rego, Inspection details.

Mandatory Technical Protocol

"What buyers look for is your truck's GCM... the combined maximum weight of the truck and trailer." – Trucks4U

Weight Ratings

GVM and GCM must be included in structured fields AND description.

Drivetrain

Engine make/model, power (kW), torque (Nm), and transmission type.

Feature (Weak) Benefit (Strong)
"Has air suspension" "Full air suspension provides superior ride quality and protects loads"
"Only 287,000km" "Below-average kilometres for age with detailed service records"

Service & Documentation

Disclose: Availability of service history, date/KMs of last service, oil sample records, and warranty status. Australian Consumer Law prohibits mis-describing vehicles—honesty prevents wasted inspections.

Good vs Bad Standards

Bad Practice

Portrait Orientation
  • ❌ Portrait orientation (Wasted screen space)
  • ❌ Cluttered yard background / bins visible
  • ❌ Standing height (makes truck small/weak)
  • ❌ Photographer's shadow or thumb in frame

Dealer Standard

Landscape
  • ✅ Full-frame landscape orientation (SEO prioritized)
  • ✅ Crouched perspective (Headlight level silhouettes)
  • ✅ Clean industrial background (Warehouse/Clean Wall)
  • ✅ Even lighting (Overcast day preferred)
